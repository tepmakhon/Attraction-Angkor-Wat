About Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, located in Siem Reap, Cambodia, is the largest religious monument in the world. Built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II, it was originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu but later became a Buddhist temple.

Known for its grand towers, intricate carvings, and vast reflection pools, Angkor Wat stands as the pinnacle of Khmer architecture. Its design symbolizes Mount Meru, the home of the gods, surrounded by the ocean represented by the moat.

Today, Angkor Wat is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most visited landmarks in Southeast Asia, drawing millions of visitors each year who come to experience its sacred sunrise views.

Interesting Facts About Angkor Wat

1. Largest Temple Complex

Angkor Wat covers more than 400 acres, making it the largest religious monument in the world.

2. Architectural Perfection

Its symmetry and alignment with celestial events show the advanced astronomical knowledge of ancient Khmer architects.

3. National Symbol

Angkor Wat is proudly featured on Cambodia’s national flag — a unique honor for a monument.

4. Thousands of Carvings

The temple walls are adorned with over 3,000 heavenly dancers known as Apsaras, symbolizing divine beauty.

5. Sunrise Experience

The sunrise over Angkor Wat’s towers is one of the most photographed scenes in the world.
